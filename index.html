Mount Washington

Mount Washington, called Agiocochook by some Native American tribes, is the highest peak in the Northeastern United States at 6,288.2 ft (1,916.6 m) and the most topographically prominent mountain east of the Mississippi River.

On the trails, it's really not time to improvise! It is therefore essential to have the necessary equipment to ensure its safety in the mountains. The best way to have a safe and enjoyable hike is to find out before you leave. Fortunately, there are several documents on the market that will allow you to supplement (if necessary) the information on this site. There are also several references on the web that can help us assess the state of the trails. In this section, you will find a list of different books that I use regularly to plan my adventures. For those who own a GPS, I suggest you take a look in my collection of tracks. You will be able to download free of charge the different routes of my adventures.
